Checkers Robot Arm

Led the mechanical design of a Robotic Arm capable of playing checkers.
Fully designed in SolidWorks with 4 degrees of freedom.
Utilizes stepper and servo motors while incorporating gearboxes and belt systems to enhance torque.

Gripper

The gripper incorporates three prongs each with their own gearing connected to a central SG90 servo.

Arm Segment

Elbow joint utilizes a 40:1 gearbox (same as the shoulder). The wrist joint free rotates with gravity so save on weight and reduce complexity of the homing system.

Shoulder Joint

Utilizes a gearbox with a 40:1 transmission ratio (same as the elbow joint).

Base

Incorporates a belt system with a 3:1 transmission ratio. A very large bearing (probably belongs in a car) sits on the bottom of the shaft which rotates the arm.

Design Evolution

Preliminary Design

This preliminary design was drafted in Solidworks. Its main purpose was to get us a rough idea of the size and functionality of the arms components. By creating a checkers board within Solidworks I was able to correctly scale the arm to accomidate. At this stage the concept for the gripper design was still being decided upon.
